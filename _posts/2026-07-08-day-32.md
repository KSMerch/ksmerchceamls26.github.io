what_i_learned: |
  Today, I spent time thinking about potential ways to improve the fairness of our models and reduce the performance disparities identified during our fairness audits. 
  After comparing the 1D CNN and Hybrid CNN-Transformer models, I began exploring different bias mitigation strategies that could help create a more equitable model 
  across demographic groups. One approach I investigated was sampling techniques, where underrepresented or underperforming groups could receive additional emphasis 
  during training. I learned that if certain groups have fewer examples or consistently receive worse predictions, adjusting how the training data is sampled may 
  help the model learn more effectively from those populations. This could potentially reduce performance gaps and improve fairness without requiring major changes 
  to the model architecture. I also looked into other bias mitigation techniques, such as weighting strategies that assign greater importance to errors made on 
  underperforming groups. By increasing the impact of these errors during training, the model may be encouraged to pay more attention to populations where performance
  is currently weaker. Exploring these ideas helped me better understand how researchers move beyond simply identifying bias and begin developing methods to address it.
