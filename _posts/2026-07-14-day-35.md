what_i_learned: |
  Today, I focused on creating our group's elevator pitch by helping film, act in, and edit the final video. Throughout the process, I learned more about how much 
  planning and coordination goes into producing a short presentation. Even though an elevator pitch is only a brief summary of a project, it takes careful thought to 
  decide what information is most important and how to communicate it effectively. While filming, I learned the importance of speaking clearly, maintaining confidence, 
  and presenting information in a way that is engaging for the audience. Since our project involves technical concepts such as machine learning, ECG classification, 
  and fairness analysis, we had to think carefully about how to explain our research in a way that could be understood by people who may not have a technical background.
  During the editing process, I learned more about organizing video clips, improving transitions, and creating a polished final product. Editing required attention to 
  detail because we wanted the video to be both professional and concise. Overall, today helped me develop skills in communication, collaboration, and presentation 
  while continuing to share the work we have been doing throughout the research program.

  Today was a fun change of pace from our usual coding and research activities. While creating the elevator pitch was still related to our project, it allowed me to

  aspect of the production. One thing that stood out to me was how challenging it can be to explain complex research in a short amount of time. We had to carefully

  we wanted viewers to take away from the presentation. I also enjoyed the collaborative nature of the activity. Working together to film scenes, act out different 
  parts, and edit the final video helped strengthen our teamwork and made the project feel more engaging. Overall, I felt productive and accomplished by the end of
