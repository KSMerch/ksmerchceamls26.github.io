what_i_learned: |
  Today, we focused on developing a hybrid CNN-Transformer model for our project. We started by discussing the paper "Attention Is All You Need" and learning about transformers. I learned that unlike traditional 
  neural networks that process information sequentially, transformers use an attention mechanism that helps the model determine which parts of the input are most important when making predictions. This approach has 
  become very influential in modern AI and is the foundation for many large language models. We then explored how we could combine Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and transformers into a single hybrid model. 
  I learned that CNNs are effective at identifying local patterns in data, such as shapes and features within ECG signals, while transformers are good at understanding larger relationships and connections across the 
  entire signal. By combining both approaches, the model may be able to take advantage of the strengths of each architecture. We also continued our work on literature reviews. During this session, we discussed how AI 
  tools can support the research process by helping researchers find relevant sources, generate citations, and compare findings across multiple studies. I learned that AI can be a useful tool for organizing and 
  analyzing information, but researchers still need to carefully evaluate sources and verify the accuracy of the information they use. Overall, today helped me learn more about advanced machine learning architectures
  while also showing how AI can assist with academic research.
