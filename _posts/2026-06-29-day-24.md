what_i_learned: |
  Today was the Mid-Summer Symposium, where my group presented the progress we have made on our research project so far. We shared our work on ECG classification, machine learning, and fairness analysis, 
  explaining the goals of our project, the methods we have used, and the results we have obtained up to this point. Presenting our research gave me valuable experience communicating technical information to an 
  audience and answering questions about our work. In addition to presenting, I had the opportunity to learn about the projects being conducted by other research groups in the program. One project focused on 
  developing Edge AI applications for human-robot collaboration in LEGO assembling operations. I learned how AI can be deployed directly on devices to assist with real-time decision-making and collaboration 
  between humans and robots in manufacturing-style tasks. Another project explored the use of AI for post-disaster building damage assessment. This research demonstrated how machine learning and computer vision 
  can help evaluate structural damage more quickly after natural disasters, potentially supporting emergency response efforts and recovery planning. I also learned about a project titled "Optimization Driven Machine 
  Learning Framework for Predicting Respiratory Health Risks Using Urban Environmental Data: Baltimore as a Case Study." This project used environmental and urban data to predict respiratory health risks, showing 
  how machine learning can be applied to public health challenges. Overall, the symposium exposed me to a wide variety of ways that AI and engineering can be used to solve real-world problems across different fields.
