what_i_learned: |
  Today was mostly a lab-focused day, and I spent a lot of time learning more about the research side of our project. We continued working on our literature reviews 
  and discussed background statements. I learned about identifying gaps in existing research and why reviewing other people's work is important. 
  Looking at previous studies helps researchers understand what has already been discovered, what questions remain unanswered, and how their own work can contribute 
  something new. During lab time, we continued working on our dataset setup. We realized that waiting nearly two hours for the dataset to install each time was 
  not an efficient use of time, so we began exploring alternative methods that could speed up the process. While waiting, I learned more about PyTorch, specifically 
  1D Convolution and how it can be used to analyze sequential data such as ECG signals. Since ECG recordings are essentially electrical signals over time, 
  understanding this concept helped me see how machine learning models can detect patterns in heart activity. We also reviewed data preparation code for our project. 
  I learned how raw ECG recordings are transformed into a format that an AI model can use. The code loads patient information and diagnoses, groups medical conditions 
  into five main categories, and converts them into labels that the model can understand. It then splits the data into training, validation, and test sets so that the 
  model can be trained and evaluated fairly. Finally, the code packages the ECG signals, patient information, and labels into batches that can be efficiently processed 
  by PyTorch. This helped me understand how much preparation is required before a machine learning model can even begin training.

  an AI model can actually learn from. Even though some of the code was complex, I felt like I understood more of the workflow than I would have a week ago. Overall, 
  I left feeling more confident in my understanding of both the research process and the technical side of our project.
