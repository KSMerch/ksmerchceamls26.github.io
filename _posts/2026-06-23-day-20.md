what_i_learned: |
  Today, I spent time improving the training code for our ECG classification model. One of the changes I made was adding more detailed output during training, 
  including information about elapsed time and a progress bar. This helped make the training process easier to monitor and provided a better sense of how long each 
  run would take. Since model training can take a significant amount of time, having clearer feedback from the code makes it easier to track progress and identify 
  potential issues. I also began learning about fairness audits, which will be an important part of Phase II of our project. I learned that a fairness audit involves 
  evaluating whether a machine learning model performs differently across demographic groups, such as different age ranges or sexes. Rather than only looking at 
  overall performance metrics, fairness audits examine whether certain groups experience higher rates of errors or receive less accurate predictions. We discussed how 
  fairness audits can be implemented in code by evaluating model predictions separately for different groups and comparing metrics such as accuracy, false positive 
  rates, and false negative rates. This helped me better understand how researchers move beyond measuring overall model performance and begin investigating whether 
  a model behaves fairly across diverse populations.

reflection: |
  Today felt productive because I was able to contribute directly to the project's code while also learning about the next phase of our research. Updating the training 
  output may seem like a small change, but it made the program much easier to follow and gave me a better understanding of what was happening during long training 
  sessions. It was satisfying to make an improvement that will be useful for future experiments. I also found the fairness audit discussion especially interesting 
  because it connects directly to the main goal of our project. Up until now, much of our work has focused on preparing data, training models, and learning evaluation 
  metrics. Learning about fairness audits helped me see how those pieces will be used to answer the larger research question about whether the model performs 
  differently across groups. Overall, I felt like today marked a transition from simply building a model to beginning the process of analyzing and interpreting its 
  behavior. That makes the project feel more meaningful because we are moving closer to investigating the fairness concerns that originally motivated the research.
