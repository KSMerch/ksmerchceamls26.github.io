what_i_learned: |
  Today, I worked on modifying our machine learning code to create a new version of the model that applies different weights to demographic groups during training. 
  The goal of this change was to investigate whether emphasizing groups that previously underperformed could improve both fairness and overall model performance. 
  As I implemented the weighted model, I learned more about how weighting strategies can influence the training process. Instead of treating every training example 
  equally, weighted approaches allow the model to place greater importance on certain samples when calculating loss and updating its parameters. This can help address 
  situations where some groups are underrepresented or experience lower performance than others. After integrating the weighting strategy into the code, I prepared 
  the model for testing and comparison against our previous models. The purpose of this experiment is to determine whether the weighted approach can reduce performance 
  disparities between demographic groups while maintaining strong classification accuracy. This helped me better understand how researchers move from identifying bias 
  to actively testing potential solutions.
