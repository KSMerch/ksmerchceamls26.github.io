what_i_learned: |
  Today, we attended a lecture series focused on artificial intelligence and its role in society. One of the topics we discussed was the different levels of AI 
  automation. I learned that automation exists on a spectrum, ranging from systems where humans maintain complete control and AI simply assists with tasks, 
  to highly automated systems where AI can perform most operations independently while humans primarily supervise. Looking at these different levels helped me 
  understand how AI can be integrated into a variety of environments, from simple tools to large-scale automated systems. We also discussed the ethics of AI and 
  some of the challenges that come with increasing automation. I learned about the importance of considering fairness, accountability, transparency, and the 
  potential impacts that AI systems can have on individuals and society. This connected well to my project because fairness and bias are important concerns when 
  using AI in healthcare applications. Later in the day, we reviewed the Overleaf template that we will use for our final research paper. I learned more about the 
  engineering paper format and how professional research papers are organized. The template included sections for the abstract, introduction, methodology, results, 
  and conclusions. Reviewing the structure gave me a better understanding of how we will present and communicate our research findings at the end of the program.
